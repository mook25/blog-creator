What is Bitcoin? A Complete Guide for Beginners

Beginner Friendly

Have you heard about Bitcoin but aren't quite sure what it actually is? You might be wondering, “I know it's digital money, but how is it different from regular money?” This guide walks you through the core ideas and safe storage methods in straightforward terms.

What is Bitcoin? A New Currency for the Digital Age

Key Summary: Bitcoin is the world's first cryptocurrency created by Satoshi Nakamoto in 2009—a digital currency that enables transactions without the need for governments or banks.

Bitcoin is a revolutionary digital currency introduced in 2009 by the mysterious developer “Satoshi Nakamoto.” Unlike traditional currencies, Bitcoin has no physical form and exists only as computer code.

Its most important feature is that there's no central authority. While the Korean won is issued by the Bank of Korea and the US dollar by the Federal Reserve, Bitcoin is managed collectively by a worldwide network of computers.

What are the Key Differences Between Regular Money and Bitcoin?

Key Summary: While central banks can control the supply of regular currencies, Bitcoin has a predetermined limit of 21 million coins total.

Differences in Issuance

Central banks can issue additional regular currency as needed. In contrast, Bitcoin has a capped supply of 21 million coins, ensuring scarcity.

Transaction Approval Methods

When you send money through a bank account, the bank verifies and approves the transaction. Bitcoin transactions are approved through a mining process where thousands of computers worldwide verify transactions.

Transaction Time and Fees

International wire transfers through banks typically take 1–3 days, but Bitcoin can be sent anywhere in the world within an average of about 10 minutes (one block).

How Does Bitcoin Enable Safe Transactions?

Key Summary: Bitcoin uses blockchain technology—a distributed ledger system that records all transactions transparently and securely.

The core technology behind Bitcoin is the blockchain. Simply put, it's a system where a massive ledger containing all transaction records is stored identically on computers worldwide.

How Blockchain Works

  1. A wants to send Bitcoin to B
  2. This transaction information is broadcast to the network
  3. Miners worldwide verify the transaction's validity
  4. Once verified, the transaction is recorded in a block
  5. The new block is linked to the existing chain

Once recorded, transactions are nearly impossible to change or delete because identical records are stored on thousands of computers worldwide.

How to Store Bitcoin Safely?

Key Summary: Bitcoin is most secure when stored in a cold wallet—an offline storage device.

Hot Wallets vs Cold Wallets

  • Hot Wallets: Connected to the internet (exchanges, mobile apps).
  • Cold Wallets: Completely offline hardware devices.

There have been numerous cases where major exchanges were hacked, resulting in large amounts of Bitcoin being stolen. By contrast, there are virtually no cases of Bitcoin stored in personal cold wallets being compromised when used properly.

Advantages of D’CENT Cold Wallets

D’CENT cold wallets store private keys in a hardware environment isolated from the internet. They only connect briefly during transactions, reducing remote attack surfaces for private key extraction.

Conclusion

Bitcoin represents more than just digital currency—it presents a new paradigm for financial systems. While it enables safe and transparent transactions without central authorities, secure storage is paramount.

If you're considering Bitcoin investment, start by establishing a secure storage environment first. Taking your first step with a reliable cold wallet sets a strong foundation for long-term self-custody.
